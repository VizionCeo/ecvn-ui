üìã Understanding Usage Rights
üí° Why Usage Rights Matter
Usage rights determine where and how a creative's work can be shown after delivery. Creatives price their work partly based on how broadly it will be used ‚Äî a video shown to your 5K Instagram followers requires less licensing value than one running as a national TV ad. Being upfront about your intended use ensures both sides agree on fair compensation and avoids disputes after delivery.
üí∞ How This Affects Pricing Negotiations
Usage rights are separate from production fees. A creative might charge $3,000 to produce your video, but the license to use that footage for paid advertising is an additional fee on top. Here's how that typically plays out:

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions
What if my plans change after I post the project? ‚ñæ
You can update your usage tier at any time before agreeing to a proposal. If your plans change mid-production, you'll need to renegotiate usage terms with your creative ‚Äî most professionals handle this routinely through a simple contract amendment.
I'm not planning to run ads now, but might later. Which do I choose? ‚ñæ
Choose Organic Social for now and negotiate a usage upgrade option in your contract. Many creatives will include a clause like "Paid advertising rights available for an additional 25% of the production fee if exercised within 12 months." This gives you flexibility without paying upfront for rights you may not use.
Does this affect who owns the final video? ‚ñæ
Usage rights and ownership are different. Unless your contract specifically states a full buyout or transfer of copyright, the creative typically retains copyright and grants you a license to use the work. "All Rights & Commercial" tiers often come with a copyright transfer or buyout ‚Äî confirm this explicitly in your contract. Always have contracts reviewed by a music industry lawyer for high-value projects.
What about using the video as part of a brand partnership or sync deal? ‚ñæ
If a brand is funding or co-sponsoring your video, or if you plan to license the music video to another brand, you'll need at least the Paid Advertising tier. If the video will be used in commercials or licensed to third parties beyond your direct use, select All Rights & Commercial and negotiate a full scope of use in your contract.
